# tvcask positioning

This guide keeps the web app, mobile app, App Store listing, and public communication on the same story.

## The position

tvcask is the independent home for your watch history. It preserves what you have watched, helps you continue without losing your place, and keeps your library with you across web and mobile.

The name carries the product idea. A cask keeps something valuable over time. tvcask keeps a person’s viewing history, progress, favorites, and lists.

TV Time import is the launch story. It is a strong reason to choose tvcask now, but it is not the whole identity. The long-term product is for anyone who wants a dependable, personal way to track shows and movies.

## Master tagline

> Your watch history, kept.

Use this as the primary line on the website, social profiles, launch graphics, and the mobile product page.

This line makes the name meaningful and gives tvcask a position beyond generic tracking:

- Your watch history names the valuable thing people bring to the product
- Kept expresses preservation, trust, continuity, and the cask metaphor

Pair it with the product promise when more explanation is needed:

> Pick up where you left off.

## One sentence

tvcask keeps your complete watch history in one place and helps you pick up exactly where you left off.

## Short product description

Track shows and movies, preserve every episode, and bring your TV Time history with you. Your library, favorites, lists, and progress stay together across web and mobile.

## Message hierarchy

### 1. Preserve your history

Years of watched episodes, movies, favorites, and lists should not disappear with an app. tvcask gives that history a dependable home.

### 2. Continue without starting over

Import first, then return to everyday tracking. tvcask remembers your progress and shows you what comes next.

### 3. One library everywhere

The web and mobile apps are two ways into the same account. Progress made on one belongs on the other.

### 4. Community with a solid foundation

Social activity, reactions, comments, and sharing should build on a private library users can already trust.

## Audience

The immediate audience is people leaving TV Time before its July 15, 2026 shutdown. They are anxious about losing years of personal history and need a migration they can trust.

The lasting audience is anyone who tracks shows and movies and wants a product that values continuity, clarity, and ownership.

## Founder story

Use the founder story when trust and motivation matter:

> I used TV Time too. When its shutdown put years of watch history at risk, I built tvcask so people could move their data, keep their progress, and continue watching.

The story should sound personal and direct. It explains why preservation came first, why the web app shipped first, and why mobile follows. It should support the product promise without making tvcask sound temporary.

## TV Time import language

Use the official TV Time GDPR export as the primary path:

> Download your official TV Time GDPR export and upload the ZIP without extracting it.

Present TV Time Out as the supported alternative for people who already used the browser extension:

> Already exported with TV Time Out? Upload the ZIP created by the Chrome extension. There is no need to export again.

When space is limited:

> Import your official TV Time export or TV Time Out ZIP.

Describe what transfers with concrete terms: shows, movies, watched episodes, favorites, and custom lists. Do not promise that every title will match. TV Time and tvcask use different catalog identifiers, so incomplete or conflicting titles may be skipped rather than linked to the wrong item.

The public import page uses this search title and headline:

> TV Time Alternative: Import Your Watch History | tvcask

> Move your TV Time watch history to tvcask

Use “TV Time alternative” on the focused import page and in search descriptions. Do not make it the master brand position. The lasting position remains the independent home for a person’s watch history.

## Migration campaign

During the TV Time shutdown window, use a direct and time-specific campaign line:

> Save your TV Time history before July 15.

Here, save is the right verb because the action is urgent and happens once. Keep remains the brand verb because it describes the lasting relationship after import.

## Voice

Write like a person who understands what it feels like to lose a familiar product.

- Be calm, direct, and reassuring
- Prefer concrete words such as history, episodes, progress, favorites, and lists
- Lead with what the user keeps or can do
- Use keep for the brand promise and save for urgent migration calls
- Use the solo founder perspective where it builds trust
- Keep urgency factual and tied to July 15, 2026

Avoid inflated language, generic claims, and startup slogans that could describe any entertainment app. Do not call tvcask revolutionary, ultimate, seamless, or all in one. Avoid defining the product only as a TV Time clone or replacement.

## Naming

Write the brand as `tvcask` in running copy. Use `tvcask for iPhone` when naming the native app in marketing. Use `tvcask web app` only when the platform distinction matters.

Keep `tvcask` lowercase at the beginning of a sentence, in page titles, in App Store fields, and in social profiles. Do not alternate between `tvcask`, `Tvcask`, and `TV Cask`. Search engines do not need a capitalized variant, and one spelling keeps the wordmark and product name aligned.
